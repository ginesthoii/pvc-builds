# PVC Garden Drip Irrigation System
Rigid Manifold Build (3/4" Schedule 40)

## Overview

This document describes a durable drip irrigation system built using 3/4 inch Schedule 40 PVC pipe. Water is distributed through small drilled holes along the pipe, forming a rigid drip manifold.

The system is designed to be serviceable, low cost, gravity compatible, and expandable. It avoids proprietary drip components and relies on standard hardware-store parts.

This is a drilled-manifold system, not commercial drip tubing.

## Materials

### Pipe and Fittings

3/4 inch Schedule 40 PVC pipe  
3/4 inch PVC slip unions  
3/4 inch PVC 90 degree elbow  
3/4 inch PVC end cap  
3/4 inch female hose thread to slip adapter  

### Tools and Consumables

PVC primer  
PVC cement  
1/16 inch drill bit  
Drill or impact driver  
PVC pipe cutter or saw  
Marker  
Rag or paper towels  

### Paint (UV Protection)

Exterior spray paint rated for plastic or exterior latex house paint.

Recommended colors:
Flat or satin green (garden blend-in)
Tan or earth tones
Light gray

Avoid dark glossy colors in full sun due to heat buildup.

## System Concept

Water enters the system from a garden hose or gravity-fed source and flows through a rigid PVC manifold. Small drilled holes allow water to drip directly into the soil along the run.

Slip unions allow the system to be disassembled for flushing, repair, or extension without cutting pipe.

The system operates best at low pressure and is compatible with gravity-fed tanks or regulated hose pressure.

## Layout Planning

All components should be dry-fit before cutting or gluing.

Decide the total run length, where turns are needed, and where unions will be placed. Unions are strongly recommended immediately after the hose adapter, before the drilled drip section, and before the end cap.

Mark pipe lengths and hole spacing before drilling.

## Cutting the Pipe

Cut the PVC pipe to length using a pipe cutter or saw. Cuts should be square. Remove internal burrs to prevent debris accumulation.

Dry-fit all parts again after cutting.

## Drilling the Drip Holes

Use a 1/16 inch drill bit.

Drill holes along the pipe where water is needed. Typical spacing is 6 to 12 inches depending on plant layout.

Drill holes on the side of the pipe or slightly downward. Do not drill holes on the top of the pipe.

Start with fewer holes. Additional holes can be added later to increase flow.

Shake or rinse the pipe to remove PVC shavings after drilling.

## Gluing the System

Glue the system in stages.

For each joint:
Clean pipe and fitting surfaces
Apply PVC primer to both
Apply PVC cement to both
Insert fully and hold for 5 to 10 seconds
Wipe excess cement

Only glue slip joints. Do not glue union collars.

### Recommended Glue Order

Hose adapter to union  
Union to straight pipe  
Straight pipe to elbow  
Elbow to drilled pipe section  
Drilled section to union  
Union to final pipe section  
Final pipe section to end cap  

Allow all joints to cure fully before pressurizing.

## End Cap Options

A glued end cap permanently seals the system.

An optional upgrade is to use a threaded adapter and removable plug instead of a glued end cap. This allows the system to be opened and flushed without cutting pipe.

## Installation

Place the PVC line along the garden bed, row, or planting area. Support with stakes if needed.

Connect the water source and slowly turn on the water.

## Testing and Adjustment

Check all joints for leaks.

Observe drip output along the line.

If flow is too strong, reduce pressure or drill fewer holes. If flow is too weak, add holes or raise the gravity source slightly.

Allow the system to run several minutes before final inspection.

## Painting the PVC (UV Protection)

Exposed PVC should be painted to protect it from ultraviolet light.

Unpainted PVC degrades over time in direct sunlight. The surface becomes chalky and brittle, and glued joints age faster. Painting significantly extends the lifespan of the system.

Paint only after all joints are glued, cured, and leak-tested.

Suitable paints include exterior spray paint rated for plastic or exterior latex house paint. Flat or satin finishes are preferred.

Recommended colors include green to blend with vegetation, tan or earth tones for soil areas, and light gray for neutral visibility.

Before painting, wipe dirt and dust from the pipe. Light scuffing is optional. Apply one to two thin coats.

Do not paint pipe ends or fitting interiors before gluing. Paint interferes with PVC primer and cement.

## Maintenance

Periodically disconnect unions and flush the line.

Clear clogged drip holes using the drill bit.

Before freezing weather, drain the system completely and disconnect if possible.

Inspect paint yearly and touch up areas with heavy sun exposure.

## Notes

This system prioritizes durability and serviceability over precision flow control.

The PVC backbone can later support filters, pressure regulators, micro-tubing, or automated valves without replacement.
